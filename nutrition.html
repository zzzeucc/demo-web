Essential Nutrients for Cats

Understanding what your cat needs for optimal health and vitality

ü•©

High-Quality Protein

Cats are obligate carnivores and require high-quality animal protein for muscle development and maintenance.

  • Chicken, turkey, fish, and beef
  • Essential amino acids like taurine
  • At least 26% protein in dry food
  • Higher protein needs for kittens and seniors
üêæ
ü•ë

Healthy Fats

Essential fatty acids support skin health, coat shine, and overall cellular function.

  • Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids
  • Fish oil and chicken fat
  • 9% minimum fat content
  • Important for vitamin absorption
üêæ
üíß

Proper Hydration

Cats have a low thirst drive and need adequate moisture in their diet to prevent health issues.

  • Fresh water available 24/7
  • Wet food provides extra moisture
  • Consider water fountains
  • Monitor for signs of dehydration
üêæ
üíä

Vitamins & Minerals

Essential micronutrients support immune function, bone health, and metabolic processes.

  • Vitamin A for vision and immunity
  • B-vitamins for energy metabolism
  • Calcium and phosphorus for bones
  • Taurine for heart and eye health

‚ö†Ô∏è Foods to Avoid

These foods can be toxic or harmful to cats and should never be given:

  • Chocolate, coffee, and caffeine
  • Onions, garlic, and chives
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Alcohol and raw dough
  • Raw eggs and raw fish
  • Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
  • Lily plants (extremely toxic)
