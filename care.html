Master the art of keeping your feline friend healthy, happy, and well-groomed with our comprehensive care instructions and expert tips.

Grooming Essentials

Keep your cat looking and feeling their best

1

Brushing

Brush your cat daily if long-haired, 2-3 times weekly for short-haired cats. Use a slicker brush for tangles and a bristle brush for finishing. Always brush in the direction of hair growth.

2

Nail Trimming

Trim nails every 2-3 weeks using cat nail clippers. Only cut the white tip, avoiding the pink quick. Hold paw gently and press pad to extend claws.

3

Ear Cleaning

Check ears weekly for dirt, wax, or odor. Clean with cotton ball and cat ear cleaner. Never use cotton swabs deep in the ear canal.

4

Dental Care

Brush teeth 2-3 times weekly with cat toothpaste and soft brush. Start slowly to get your cat accustomed to the process.

5

Eye Care

Wipe away discharge with damp, clean cloth. Use separate cloth for each eye. Contact vet if discharge is colored or excessive.

6

Bathing (When Needed)

Most cats don't need baths unless very dirty or have skin conditions. Use lukewarm water and cat-specific shampoo. Rinse thoroughly.

Pro Grooming Tips

Start grooming routines when your cat is young to get them used to handling. Always reward with treats and praise. If your cat is resistant, try short sessions and gradually increase duration. For matted fur, use a dematting comb or consider professional grooming.

Health & Wellness

Essential health care for your feline companion

1

Regular Vet Checkups

Schedule annual wellness exams for adult cats, bi-annual for seniors (7+). Kittens need multiple visits in their first year for vaccinations and spaying/neutering.

2

Vaccinations

Keep up with core vaccines (rabies, FVRCP) and discuss lifestyle-based vaccines with your vet. Maintain vaccination records and follow recommended schedules.

3

Parasite Prevention

Use monthly flea/tick prevention and regular deworming as recommended by your vet. Check for signs of parasites during grooming sessions.

4

Weight Management

Monitor your cat's weight regularly. Overweight cats are prone to diabetes, arthritis, and other health issues. Adjust food portions and increase play time as needed.

Emergency Signs - Contact Your Vet Immediately

Difficulty breathing, not eating for 24+ hours, vomiting repeatedly, difficulty urinating, lethargy, hiding, sudden behavior changes, seizures, or any signs of pain or distress.

Daily Care Routine

Creating a healthy daily environment

1

Litter Box Maintenance

Scoop daily, completely change litter weekly. Provide one box per cat plus one extra. Keep boxes clean and in quiet, accessible locations.

2

Fresh Water & Food

Provide fresh water daily in clean bowls. Feed measured portions of high-quality cat food at consistent times. Wash food and water bowls regularly.

3

Exercise & Play

Engage in 10-15 minutes of active play twice daily. Use toys that mimic hunting behavior like feather wands, laser pointers, and interactive toys.

4

Environmental Enrichment

Provide scratching posts, climbing trees, window perches, and hiding spots. Rotate toys weekly to maintain interest and prevent boredom.
